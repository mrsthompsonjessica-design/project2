  • 10 pound bone-in cooked ham
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup dark brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup honey
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 5 cloves garlic, smashed

So how exactly is it made?

For the most part, you will be cooking your ham according to the package directions, usually 15 minutes per pound.

  1. Preheat oven to 275 degrees. Remove any plastic packaging or netting from the ham. Be sure to let the ham come to room temperature before cooking.
  2. Place your ham cut side down in a deep baking pan with a rack in the bottom. Add the Â½ cup of water to the bottom of the pan and tent with foil.
  3. Place in the bottom third of the oven and cook until there is 20 minutes remaining on the cook time.
  4. Meanwhile, heat the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat until golden brown.
  5. Add the brown sugar, mustard, honey, cinnamon, and cloves, stirring well to combine and dissolve completely (about 2 minutes).
  6. Reduce heat to low and add in the smashed garlic, allowing it to become fragrant, cooking for about one to two minutes until the glaze begins to simmer.
  7. Set aside and let cool to lukewarm. The glaze should be the consistencyÂ of room temperature honey and easily coat a spoon.
  8. With 20 minutes remaining, pull the ham out and turn on its side, separating the slices slightly. Pour and brush your glaze all over the tops and sides, brushing in between the folds.
  9. Place back in the oven, uncovered, for the remainder of the cookÂ time.
